# Topic of Research
Sharpening Satellite Heat Maps using Physics and AI:
The research focuses on making blurry satellite temperature images of cities much sharper and more detailed. It does this by combining different satellite images with actual physics laws about how heat moves on the ground. This helps map "urban heat islands" (areas in cities that get dangerously hot).

High-resolution output from coarse data is crucial in fields like climate science and remote sensing. Recent work explores combining physical knowledge and multiple data modalities to improve downscaling (super-resolution). One strategy is physics-informed learning, which enforces conservation laws or PDEs within neural networks. For example, Harder et al. embed a “renormalization” layer to strictly enforce mass conservation in climate downscaling, yielding higher accuracy than unconstrained models. Another strategy is multimodal guidance, where auxiliary data (e.g. segmentation maps, depth, or text) supply rich context. For instance, segmentation maps have been used to guide image super-resolution, producing sharp, noise-free results. A recent diffusion-based SR model conditions on text captions, depth and segmentation to reduce hallucinations and better align fine details. Overall, surveys note that multimodal guidance and physics-informed architectures are emerging frontiers in super-resolution research.
