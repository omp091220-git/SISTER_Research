AI Sharpens City Heat Maps: Physics-Informed Geospatial Downscaling
Welcome to the overview document for the AI Sharpens City Heat Maps research framework. This project explores how combining artificial intelligence with physical laws can improve the clarity of urban temperature data, allowing scientists and planners to effectively map out localized climate risks.

🌍 Core Theme

Climate + Geospatial AI: Using artificial intelligence to study climate change and geography.

🔬 Topic of Research

Sharpening Satellite Heat Maps Using Physics and AI: The main research objective focuses on transforming blurry satellite temperature images of cities into much sharper, highly detailed versions.


Methodology: The system achieves this by combining different types of satellite imagery with actual laws of physics that describe how heat moves across the ground.


Impact: This approach helps map "urban heat islands," identifying specific areas within cities that get dangerously hot.

❓ Core Research Questions

Physics-Constraints vs. Basic Machine Learning Can an AI that is forced to follow physics laws predict city temperatures at a highly detailed scale (10 to 30 meters) more accurately than standard AI models that only guess based on patterns? 


Multimodal Data Fusion How much does mixing different types of satellite data (like plant health, land elevation, and surface types) improve these temperature maps, and which data type helps the most when clouds are blocking the satellite's view? 


Feature-Specific Sensitivity Does teaching the AI specific details about city features (like roofs, roads, and trees) reduce errors in extra-hot spots compared to an AI model that treats every surface the exact same way?
